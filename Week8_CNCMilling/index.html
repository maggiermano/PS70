Week 8: CNC Milling

This week the assignment was to design something and make it using CNC. After this we had to showcase post-processing of a design using fabrication processes.

1. An ode to USAP

This week I wanted to do something a little closer to home. My mom founded a school in Zimbabwe for high-achieving, low-income students called the USAP Community School. This week I decided to make a wall decoration of Zimbabwe using pocket cuts to give a 3D effect with the logo of the school in the center of the map.

List of materials and resources used:

  • CNC ShopBot Mill
  • Autodesk Fusion 360 and Blender software for modelling

Steps for making the USAP decoration:

  1. Designing the Layout
    • I used Fusion and Blender to design the wall decoration. I used fusion to create the logo and used this Blender tutorial to convert an image of Zimbabwe to a sketch.
    • I used the offset feature in fusion to create an outer layer of the map.
  2. CNC Milling the Design
    • I used the pocket cut method with a finer end mill (1/8 inch) to get the inside lettering detail of the logo. However, the lettering for the "Community School" portion ended up being too thin to hold and broke off during the milling process.
    • I was still pleased with the results because the detail of the logo and larger lettering looks great.
    • I then cut out the map after a long pocket cut process. I used the 1/4 inch end mill. I then used the belt sander to remove wood splinters from the edges.
  3. Final Look

2. My Spidey Senses Are Tingling...

To show off some post-processing skills I also designed a Spider-man logo. The goal was to use the smaller CNC mill to create wax mold and from that use silicon to make a soft mold. The soft mold would be used to make a solid little Spidey logo from a harder material. a silicon mold.

List of materials and resources used:

  • Smaller CNC Mill
  • Autodesk Fusion 360 software for modelling

Steps for making the Spidey mold:

  1. Designing the Layout
    • I used Fusion to model design. I used the canvas mode to insert an image of the logo and then created a logo by tracing the image and using the mirror feature.
  2. CNC Milling the Design
    • I used an 1/8 inch end mill for the rough cut and planned to use a 1/16 inch end mill for the finish cut. Unfortunately my wax mold was removed from the machine before the finishing cut was achieved, and I wasn't able to achieve the same detail as the design.
    • The main outline of the spider can still be seen (see in image above), however I would love to redo the mold to see what it would have looked like with a finished cut.
  3. Silicon Molding
    • I then followed the instructions on the bottles and mixed silicon mold parts thoroughly. After this I poured the mixture into my wax mold and let it set until the next day.
  4. Metal Casting
    • I melted metal in the microwave before carefully pouring it into my set silicone mold.
    • After allowing it to cool, I removed the cast metal piece which marked the end of the post-fabrication process.
  5. Final Look
